Company Registration in Nepal

Introduction

Company registration in Nepal is managed by the Office of the Company Registrar (OCR) and is essential for operating legally. This guide provides a detailed breakdown of the types of companies you can register, required documents, registration steps, fees, and post-registration requirements.

Types of Companies

Based on Nepalese law, companies are categorized as:

  • Private Limited Company: For small to medium enterprises with limited liability.
  • Public Limited Company: Suitable for large-scale businesses with provisions for more shareholders.
  • Non-Profit Company: Organizations like NGOs operate under this type.

Company Registration Process

  1. Company Name Reservation: Submit your proposed company name for OCR approval. This may take 3-7 days.
  2. Prepare MOA and AOA: Draft the Memorandum of Association and Articles of Association detailing company objectives, management policies, and shareholder information.
  3. Document Collection and Submission: Collect notarized copies of required documents, including identity proofs of shareholders and company address proof.
  4. Application Review and Fee Payment: The OCR reviews your application. If corrections are needed, revise and resubmit. Government registration fees depend on authorized capital.
  5. Company Registration Certificate: Upon approval, OCR issues the certificate, making your company a legal entity in Nepal.
  6. Tax Registration: Obtain a Permanent Account Number (PAN) and Value Added Tax (VAT) registration from the Inland Revenue Office.

Post-Registration Compliances

  • Obtain necessary business licenses (e.g., tourism requires Ministry approval).
  • Initial OCR compliances: Document auditor appointments and update addresses.
  • Company Seal Creation: Prepare a seal with the company’s name.
  • Ward Office Registration: Register locally before obtaining PAN.
  • Permanent Account Number (PAN): Secure PAN after biometric updates.
  • Open Company Bank Account: Essential for financial transactions.
  • Annual Compliances: Maintain shareholder registers, update BoD, and file reports.

Conclusion

Registering a company in Nepal requires a step-by-step approach, from name reservation to post-registration compliance. Following each step accurately ensures a smooth start to your business venture.
